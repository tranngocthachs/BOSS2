About

BOSS2 is a streamlined rewrite of the BOSS Online Submission System.

BOSS1

The BOSS Online Submission System was a course management tool, developed by the Department of Computer Science at the University of Warwick.

BOSS allowed students to submit assignments online securely, and contained a selection of tools to allow staff to mark assignments online and to manage their modules efficiently.

Many people, both staff and students, have contributed both code and ideas to the project. There are too many to mention here, but major contributors include (in alphabetical order): Russell Boyatt, Chris Box, David Buckley, Ian Burnett, Pui-Shan Chan, Lee Earl, Nathan Griffiths, Ben Hart, Paul Isitt, Mike Joy, Raymond Lee, Michael Luck, Jonathan Pallant, Bilel Remmache, Bill Wessel, Daniel White and Shanna Xu.

BOSS2

BOSS2 is a rewrite of the BOSS Online Submission System to focus on the strengths of submission and testing.

The following people contributed their efforts to BOSS2:

  • Project oversight: Mike Joy, Russel Boyat.
  • Design and Development: David Byard.
  • Testing: George Stanley, Jonathan Priest, Nick Shrine.
  • User interface research: Peyshan Heng.

Funding

The BOSS2 project has been funded internally within the University, with assistance from both the Department of Computer Science and from the University's Research and Teaching Development Fund.
